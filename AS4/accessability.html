Accessability

Types of impairments

The types of impairments that sites need to address according to wikipedia are.
Visual, Motor and Mobility, Auditory, Seizures, and Intellectual

Guidelines to address impairments

The W3C published the Web Content Acessibility Guidelines in 1999. Tips like, "Don't rely on color alone" or "Provide equivalent alternatives to audotory and visual content" lay the foundation of building an accessibile website.
More information about the WCAG 1.0 and 2.0 can be found here.
